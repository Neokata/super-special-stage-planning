Notes / assumptions
  • Max potential income at 100% sellout:
    • Route 1 (Fri AM, 60 cars × $150) = $9,000
    • Route 2 (Sat AM, 150 cars × $75) = $11,250
    • Route 3 (Sat PM, 150 cars × $75) = $11,250
    • Route subtotal: $31,500
    • Plus $2,200 max kickoff parking + other revenue
  • No discounts. Each slot is one price. A driver taking all 3 slots pays $300 ($150 + $75 + $75). Replaces the older 3-tier $75/$60/$50 model.
  • Per-slot fill sliders scale revenue independently. So if Friday is sold out at 60 but Saturday is at 50%, each slider reflects that.
  • Friday pack model: 2 packs of 30, 2 lead + 2 sweep drivers (4 driver-pays for Friday).
  • Saturday pack model: 5 different physical routes, each with Pack A (AM) and Pack B (PM) of 15 cars. Per route: 1 lead + 1 tail. 5 routes × 2 staff = 10 driver-pays for Saturday. The same staff works both AM and PM for their route — one pay per staff per day, not per session.
  • Friday kickoff venue & city (park, 6-9 PM = 3 hrs): park rental $250/hr (1 venue), 6 park staff at $30/hr, 8 police officers at $50/hr. Venue & city subtotal = $750 + $540 + $1,200 = $2,490. Each rate / headcount / hours is editable in case quotes come in different.
  • Total weekend staff drivers: 14 (4 Fri + 10 Sat). At $300/staff = $4,200 default. Edit the "Lead + sweep driver pay" cell in Ops to model different rates.
  • Driver swag (t-shirts + windshield banners, $10 each) auto-scales with the per-slot fill sliders. At 100% sellout: 360 drivers × $20 = $7,200. Drop the sliders to model partial sellout — swag cost updates instantly.
  • Auto-linked per-attendance costs: t-shirts ($10/driver), windshield banners ($10/driver), stickers ($1/driver), raffle prizes ($1/driver), digital marketing ($1/driver), raffle ticket revenue ($2/driver). Each is qty × $/unit where qty auto-derives from the fill sliders. Staff T-shirts are qty × $/shirt where qty is the editable staff headcount (default 30, $13/shirt). Dinner send-off is modeled at $0 — drivers pay for their own meal at a coordinated restaurant or tailgate.
  • Numbers persist via localStorage on this browser only. Clearing site data wipes them — copy out to a sheet if you need a real backup. The Reset to defaults button clears saved state and restores all cells to the original placeholders.
